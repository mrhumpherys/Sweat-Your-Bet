Paul George Ruled Out Tuesday

Los Angeles Clippers forward Paul George (foot) has been ruled out for Tuesday's contest against the Magic. It marks George's second straight absence, as he also missed Monday's game against the Bucks due to a sore foot. Forward Marcus Morris Sr., guard Reggie Jackson, and guard Luke Kennard scored 20-plus points with at least four triples without George in the picture against Milwaukee. The Magic have been very generous to wing players this year, so all three are in play for DFS formats, as well as Kawhi Leonard.

Hassan Whiteside Out Monday Against Spurs

Sacramento Kings center Hassan Whiteside will miss his third straight contest on Monday against San Antonio. Whiteside has been dealing with a sore knee, though it's at least a good sign that he was initially listed as questionable. His absence will mean that Richaun Holmes will continue to dominate the frontcourt touches for Sacramento, though forwards Chimezie Metu and Chris Silva could have solid games if they see added minutes. Metu has been earning more trust with the coaching staff lately, as he has 13 points, nine rebounds, and three blocks in his last 30 minutes across two contests.

Kyle Lowry To Miss 7-10 Days Of Action

Toronto Raptors guard Kyle Lowry (foot) will be sidelined for 7-10 days, per Blake Murphy of The Athletic. Lowry was ruled out early for Wednesday's game, with head coach Nick Nurse saying that the veteran guard's toe infection \"is not going well.\" With Lowry out for a week, guards Fred VanVleet and Gary Trent Jr. will shoulder a bigger scoring load in the backcourt. Guard Rodney Hood and forward Stanley Johnson could also see expanded roles for Toronto. VanVleet and Trent should be the more reliable DFS plays of the bunch.

De'Andre Hunter Ruled Out Sunday

Atlanta Hawks forward De'Andre Hunter (knee) will not play Sunday against the Denver Nuggets. Hunter recently returned from a lengthy knee injury and is experiencing knee soreness, missing the past two games. It is possible Hunter may have returned too soon which made his knee flare up. Regardless, Hunter's absence should lead to another start for Tony Snell and more minutes for Bogdan Bogdanovic, Danilo Gallinari and Kevin Huerter. John Collins is also a big winner here and should be a popular DFS target in this one.
